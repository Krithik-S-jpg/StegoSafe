About StegoSafe

What is Steganography?

Steganography is the art of hiding information in plain sight. Unlike encryption which scrambles data, steganography conceals the existence of the message itself. StegoSafe uses Least Significant Bit (LSB) encoding to hide messages inside image files.

How it Works

StegoSafe modifies the least significant bits of image pixels to encode your message. These changes are invisible to the human eye but contain your hidden data. Your message is first encrypted with AES-256-GCM for security, then compressed to save space, and finally embedded into the image.
